The Quiet Temple Inside You

உன்னுள்ளே இருக்கும் அமைதியான கோயில்

Karthikeyan  ·  April 2025  ·  5 min read

We travel miles to find peace. We wake up before sunrise, stand in long queues, fold our hands, close our eyes — all in search of that one moment of stillness that feels like grace. Temples, rivers, mountains. We look everywhere outside ourselves.

But I have been wondering lately — what if the most sacred place is not made of stone? What if the quietest temple, the one that never closes, never crowds, never asks you to remove your footwear — is already inside you?

"உன்னை நீயே அறிந்தால்,
வேறு எங்கும் தேட வேண்டியதில்லை."
— When you know yourself, you need not search anywhere else.

The Noise We Call Living

Most of us go through the day with our minds running like a crowded bus stand — horns, announcements, strangers brushing past. We call this being busy. We wear it like a badge. But somewhere underneath all that noise, there is a version of us that is sitting very quietly, waiting to be noticed.

I felt this once, sitting alone in my room after a difficult week. No phone. No television. Just the ceiling fan and the sound of evening settling in. And for a few minutes, I felt something open up inside me — a space I had forgotten existed. It was not empty. It was full. Full of something soft and steady that I had no word for.

· · ·

What the 8th House Teaches Us

In Vedic astrology, the 8th house is the house of transformation, depth, and hidden things. People with a strong 8th house are not afraid of darkness — they are drawn to understand it. They ask the questions that others avoid. They feel things at layers that most people do not reach.

I used to think this was a burden. Now I think it is a gift. Because the people who go deep — who are willing to sit with discomfort, to feel fully, to transform — those are the ones who eventually find something real on the other side.

The quiet temple I am talking about is not found by avoiding pain. It is found by walking through it with open eyes and a willing heart.

A Simple Practice

You do not need to meditate for hours. You do not need a guru or a mantra or incense. Just try this: once a day, sit somewhere quiet for five minutes. Not to think. Not to solve. Just to notice that you exist. Notice your breathing. Notice the weight of your own presence in the room.

That noticing — that small, humble act of turning your attention inward — that is visiting the temple inside you. And if you do it enough, you will find that the peace you were looking for in faraway places was always available, right here, right now.

"மனம் அடங்கினால் — அனைத்தும் அடங்கும்."
— When the mind is still, everything is still.

I do not have all the answers. I am still learning how to visit that quiet place regularly, how to carry it with me through the noise of the day. But I know it is there. And knowing it is there makes everything feel a little lighter.

That is enough for now.
